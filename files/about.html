About Me

MY STORY

Hi, my name is Nicole and this is my story about how I became passionate about user experience design.

From 2011-2015, I studied Public Health Sciences and minored in Health Informatics (HI) at UC Irvine. I became really interested in the field of HI when I took my first Intro course at UCI. One of the topics we discussed was the issues that hospitals faced with EHR systems and the one issue that stuck out to me was how errors in these systems caused the deaths of patients. As an avid tech enthusiast, I was upset, frustrated, and quickly became curious and driven to explore technologyâ€™s role in healthcare. This was the moment it clicked in my head that what I wanted to do with the rest of my life was to discover how technology can improve and innovate healthcare and the life sciences.

In 2015, I continued my studies as a masters student of Health Informatics at University of Michigan and was exposed to a multitude of contexts in which technology, design, and healthcare intersect. I learned that, compared to other industries, healthcare is extremely complex and to design successful technological solutions in this industry that really work is challenging. I learned that core to doing so is not only understanding people, their attitudes, and their behaviors, but also the context in which people work - because in healthcare, there is rarely a one-size-fits-all solution.

MY GOAL

I believe that my purpose is to help advance healthcare through technology. I am dedicated to designing beautiful data-driven experiences in the healthcare space as I pursue my early passion as a UX researcher and designer.
